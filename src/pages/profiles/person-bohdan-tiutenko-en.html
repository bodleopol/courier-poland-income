In plain language

I am an operations leader. I like clear rules of the game, honest numbers, and people who are not afraid to leave the office for the front line. My recent years are big cities, food on the move, complex routes, and many stakeholders at once. I do not sell “leadership magic” — I prefer calm discipline: we improve what we measure.

At Rocket I grew up with tasks where every minute counts: hundreds of couriers, peak loads, expectations from customers and partners. Peru was different — another culture, another tempo, the same logic: if the kitchen and delivery breathe at different speeds, margin suffers. So I spent a lot of time between production, dispatch, and financial models so decisions did not live only in chat threads.

Today I am looking for a COO or Head of Operations role in a company where I can own the “steel” of the process — from people’s safety to unit economics. I am especially interested in foodtech, logistics, service platforms, and teams that are past the “we just grow” stage and ready to invest in systems.

How to connect

I am happy with a short intro call or an email that describes the role and scope. I do not promise a “revolution in 30 days” — I do promise clear priorities, understandable metrics, and calm communication when the plan breaks.
