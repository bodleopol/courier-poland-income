Editorial methodology

Rybezh.site adds pages about people and companies when they are useful on their own—not as filler.

1. Editorial scope

We publish specialist and company pages as a reference base. Priority goes to modern post-2000 professionals, especially programmers, engineers, executives and founders in technology-heavy sectors.

2. Profile requirements

Each profile should include a name, role, photo, professional focus, key contribution, concise biography and thematic tags. Entries with no real substance do not belong here.

3. Quality and selection

Thin pages that do not help a reader understand a person or company are not kept in the catalogue.

4. Sources and corrections

Profiles rely on public professional context, biographies and submitted editorial materials. If a profile becomes inaccurate or outdated, it should be reviewed, updated or removed.

5. Interview drill

The Interview drill is a separate reader-facing tool: it is not an editorial judgement on catalogue entries and does not affect who is listed. Ten thousand unique strings are assembled at build time from curated lists and templates (combinatorics of context, stressor, role, competency, and question archetype). To suggest wording changes to the dictionaries or templates, email jobs.r@protonmail.com with a concrete example and the fix you want.

Editorial voice

The catalogue is led by Maria Rubezh (Rybezh.site). We keep tone consistent across languages and verify facts where possible.
